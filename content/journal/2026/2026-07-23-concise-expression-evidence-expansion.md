# Concise Expression Evidence Expansion

## Summary

The established voice-eligible corpus workflow was applied to the concise-expression hypothesis across all eleven chronological batches.

## Lesson

Short text is not automatically concise evidence.

The first pilot confused generic brevity and missing context with semantic compression. A corrected prompt and human review separated:

- Complete compressed meaning from short fragments.
- Recoverable reader inference from unavailable context.
- Unnecessary elaboration from detail required by the format or audience.

## Result

Human review retained sixteen supporting candidates, three contradictory candidates, and two provisional contradictory candidates.

The strongest evidence compresses causal relationships, cultural connections, judgments, jokes, and micro-narratives. Supporting examples recur from 2009 through 2020.

EVID-002 and VAL-002 were revised to Version 2. The validation remains provisionally supported, with medium confidence inside the Facebook scope and low confidence for frequency, recent continuity, and other writing formats.
