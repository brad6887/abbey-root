# Quoted Language Validation

VAL-004 tested the stance-marker hypothesis without reusing the canonical
EVID-004 examples.

The deterministic definition removed fifteen supporting examples, seven
comparison examples, and four rejected matches from the 165 reviewed
quote-bearing candidates. The resulting holdout contained exactly 139
sources.

Sixty-one holdout posts used the two core functions, a rate of 43.88%. The 95%
Wilson interval was 35.91% to 52.19%, leaving the lower bound above the 30%
threshold.

Both subtypes replicated independently: thirty-six distancing cases and
twenty-five comic-renaming cases remained after canonical evidence was
removed.

The pattern also survived the chronology test. Core cases appeared in all four
required bands, although the count declined from twenty-five in 2009–2011 to
only three in 2018–2021. There were no holdout core cases after 2021.

Sixty ordinary quotation comparisons remained. That boundary is important
because it demonstrates that the method is not simply labeling every
quotation mark as voice evidence.

Every threshold and source-integrity check passed. HYP-004 is provisionally
supported with medium confidence inside the tested Facebook scope.

The fourth chain is now complete. The next useful step is not automatically
creating OBS-005. It is synthesizing the four validated chains into a bounded
first Voice Model draft and being explicit about what the Facebook research
does not yet establish.
