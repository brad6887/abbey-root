# Voice Analysis Artifact Migration Workflow Review

## Summary

The Voice Analysis project completed three manual artifact migrations and reviewed the resulting workflow.

The migrations demonstrated that the formal artifact lifecycle is repeatable and provides a useful structure for preserving research traceability.

## Completed Research Chains

The project now contains three complete formal research chains:

- Deadpan Delivery
- Concise Expression
- Recurring Narrative Elements

Each chain follows:

Corpus

↓

Experiment

↓

Observation

↓

Evidence

↓

Hypothesis

↓

Validation

## Research Process Insight

The existing Experiment 001 work contained significant research value before formalization.

The migration process did not recreate analysis. Instead, it transformed existing research into a structured artifact model where relationships and confidence levels are explicit.

## Framework Insight

Three migrations revealed that the repeatable portion of the process is structural.

The researcher still needs to make decisions about:

- What constitutes an observation.
- What evidence is meaningful.
- Whether a hypothesis is too broad.
- Whether validation has actually challenged the hypothesis.

These decisions should remain human-guided.

## Future Automation Direction

The next logical Abbey research capability is not automatic artifact generation.

The next useful capability is research visibility.

A future:

abbey research status

command could provide:

- Corpus status.
- Experiment status.
- Artifact chain completeness.
- Remaining migration work.

This would improve research navigation without automating interpretation.

## Next Steps

Design the Abbey Research Status command before implementing additional research automation.

The current workflow has provided enough examples to begin designing tooling based on observed requirements.
