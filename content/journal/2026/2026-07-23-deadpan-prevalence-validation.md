# Deadpan Prevalence Validation

## Summary

The deadpan hypothesis moved from candidate retrieval to representative prevalence measurement.

A deterministic 140-post sample was drawn from the corrected 1,342-post research view. Every post was annotated and human-reviewed.

## Result

Sixteen sampled posts contained a qualifying absurd-humorous premise. Eleven used deadpan delivery and five used overt or explanatory delivery.

The conditional deadpan rate is 68.75%, with a 95% Wilson interval of 44.40% to 85.84%.

The predeclared frequency threshold required the lower bound to be at least 20%. The result passes.

## Interpretation

"Frequently" is supported only within the correct denominator: Facebook posts containing a discernible absurd or impossible humorous premise.

Deadpan posts account for 7.86% of the complete sample. The result does not mean that most Facebook writing is deadpan.

## Lesson

Candidate retrieval establishes existence and recurrence. It cannot establish prevalence.

A defensible frequency claim requires:

- A representative sample.
- A denominator defined before annotation.
- A threshold defined before seeing results.
- Human-reviewed labels.
- Uncertainty reported with the point estimate.

## Next Step

Validate and commit the deadpan prevalence increment. Later prevalence studies should use independent annotation or a larger sample before raising confidence beyond medium.
