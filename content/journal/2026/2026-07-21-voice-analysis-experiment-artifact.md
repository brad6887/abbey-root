# Voice Analysis Experiment Artifact

## Summary

The Voice Analysis project moved the first research experiment from an informal directory structure into the formal artifact lifecycle.

The previous work already contained the research outputs from Experiment 001, but the experiment itself had not been represented as a first-class artifact.

## Work Completed

Created:

- `docs/research/voice-analysis/experiments/EXP-001.md`

The new experiment artifact documents:

- Objective
- Research question
- Scope
- Corpus relationship
- Methodology
- Research outputs
- Results
- Lessons learned

The experiment artifact establishes the connection between the formal corpus artifact and the existing research outputs.

## Key Finding

The existing Experiment 001 directory contained most of the required research information.

The missing piece was not additional analysis, but formal structure.

The artifact model allows existing research to be preserved while making relationships, dependencies, and provenance explicit.

## Lessons Learned

The migration process should prioritize establishing the complete artifact hierarchy before expanding research.

Creating formal parent artifacts first makes later observation, evidence, and hypothesis migration simpler and more traceable.

## Next Steps

Complete the remaining Experiment 001 alignment work.

After the foundation phase is complete, begin migrating existing Experiment 001 observations into formal research artifacts.
