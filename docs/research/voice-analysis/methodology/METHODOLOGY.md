# Voice Analysis Methodology

## Purpose

This document defines the research methodology used to understand an author's writing voice.

The methodology is designed to be:

- Evidence-based
- Reproducible
- Explainable
- Independent of AI
- Applicable to multiple authors

The objective is not simply to describe an author's style, but to develop a repeatable process for constructing an accurate and defensible voice model.

---

# Research Workflow

The methodology follows a staged workflow.

```text
Corpus Collection
        │
        ▼
Corpus Cleaning
        │
        ▼
Corpus Classification
        │
        ▼
Evidence Collection
        │
        ▼
Hypothesis Development
        │
        ▼
Validation
        │
        ▼
Voice Model
        │
        ▼
Derived Outputs
```

Each stage should produce artifacts that can be reviewed independently.

---

# Stage 1 — Corpus Collection

## Objective

Assemble a representative collection of an author's writing.

Possible sources include:

- Social media
- Blogs
- Books
- Journals
- Technical documentation
- Emails
- Interviews
- Public presentations

The corpus should span multiple years whenever possible in order to distinguish enduring characteristics from temporary changes.

Deliverable:

- Complete corpus inventory

---

# Stage 2 — Corpus Cleaning

## Objective

Extract only the author's original writing.

Remove material that does not represent the author's voice.

Examples include:

- Link previews
- HTML
- Navigation text
- Metadata
- Automatically generated content
- Duplicate entries
- Shared content written by others

Cleaning rules should be documented and reproducible.

Deliverable:

- Clean corpus

---

# Stage 3 — Corpus Classification

## Objective

Organize the corpus into meaningful categories.

Possible classifications include:

### By format

- Short posts
- Long-form articles
- Technical writing
- Narrative writing
- Dialogue

### By purpose

- Informational
- Humorous
- Reflective
- Instructional
- Personal
- Technical

### By period

- Early writing
- Middle period
- Recent writing

The classification system may evolve as research progresses.

Deliverable:

- Classified corpus

---

# Stage 4 — Evidence Collection

## Objective

Document observable characteristics of the author's writing.

Evidence consists of specific passages that support or contradict potential observations.

Evidence should avoid interpretation whenever possible.

Each piece of evidence should reference:

- Source
- Date
- Excerpt
- Observation

Deliverable:

- Evidence library

---

# Stage 5 — Hypothesis Development

## Objective

Develop testable hypotheses describing the author's writing.

Examples:

- The author usually begins with observation before opinion.
- Humor is primarily observational.
- Technical writing favors narrative explanations.

Hypotheses should include:

- Status
- Supporting evidence
- Counterexamples
- Confidence
- Open questions

No hypothesis is considered accepted until validated.

Deliverable:

- Hypothesis catalog

---

# Stage 6 — Validation

## Objective

Attempt to disprove each hypothesis.

Validation includes searching for:

- Counterexamples
- Time-based inconsistencies
- Genre differences
- Audience differences
- Situations where the hypothesis does not hold

Confidence increases only after repeated attempts to invalidate the hypothesis.

Deliverable:

- Validated hypotheses

---

# Stage 7 — Voice Model

## Objective

Construct a coherent model describing the author's writing voice.

The model should describe recurring characteristics rather than isolated behaviors.

The voice model should distinguish between:

- Core voice
- Stylistic preferences
- Evolution over time
- Context-dependent behaviors

The voice model should reference validated hypotheses rather than raw observations.

Deliverable:

- Voice Model

---

# Stage 8 — Derived Outputs

## Objective

Produce practical artifacts derived from the validated voice model.

Possible outputs include:

- Voice Profile
- Writing Manual
- Style Guide
- AI Prompting Guide
- Comparative analyses
- Research reports

These outputs are considered secondary artifacts and should never become the primary source of truth.

---

# Research Standards

## Evidence Before Conclusions

Every conclusion must be supported by documented evidence.

---

## Preserve Uncertainty

Not every observation becomes a conclusion.

Ideas remain hypotheses until validated.

---

## Record Counterexamples

Counterexamples are as valuable as supporting evidence.

They strengthen or invalidate hypotheses.

---

## Document Evolution

Authors change over time.

The methodology should distinguish:

- Core voice
- Temporary stylistic changes
- Long-term evolution

---

## Reproducibility

Another researcher following this methodology should be able to reach substantially similar conclusions from the same corpus.

---

# Role of AI

Artificial intelligence may assist with:

- Corpus processing
- Pattern discovery
- Classification
- Summarization
- Validation

However, AI is not considered an authoritative source.

All conclusions must remain traceable to the original corpus and supporting evidence.

---

# Success Criteria

The methodology is considered successful when it can:

- Produce consistent results across multiple researchers.
- Explain why conclusions were reached.
- Trace every conclusion to supporting evidence.
- Distinguish enduring voice from temporary style.
- Generalize to additional authors without modification.

The methodology—not any individual case study—is the primary deliverable of the project.
