# Report Model

## Purpose

This document defines the structure of research reports produced by the Voice Analysis framework.

Reports communicate the results of research to readers without requiring them to navigate the complete collection of observations, evidence, hypotheses, and experiments.

A report summarizes research.

It does not replace the underlying research artifacts.

---

# Relationship to the Framework

Reports are generated after research has been completed.

```text
Corpus
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Evidence
    ↓
Hypothesis
    ↓
Validation
    ↓
Voice Model
    ↓
Research Report
```

Reports present conclusions.

Research artifacts support those conclusions.

---

# Characteristics

A report should be:

- Accurate
- Traceable
- Evidence-based
- Understandable
- Versioned
- Reproducible

Reports should avoid speculation beyond the available evidence.

---

# Recommended Structure

## Executive Summary

A concise overview of the research.

Answer:

- What was studied?
- Why was it studied?
- What was discovered?

---

## Research Objective

Describe the purpose of the research.

---

## Corpus

Summarize:

- Source material
- Scope
- Time period
- Limitations

---

## Methodology

Briefly explain how the Voice Analysis methodology was applied.

---

## Significant Findings

Summarize the most important validated conclusions.

Each finding should reference supporting hypotheses.

---

## Supporting Evidence

Identify the most important evidence supporting the findings.

The report should summarize rather than duplicate evidence.

---

## Exceptions

Document important exceptions or limitations.

Research is strengthened by acknowledging uncertainty.

---

## Confidence

Describe overall confidence in the findings.

Confidence should reflect:

- Corpus quality
- Evidence quality
- Validation results

---

## Open Questions

Identify areas requiring additional research.

---

## Future Work

Recommend logical follow-up experiments.

---

## References

Reference supporting:

- Voice Models
- Experiments
- Hypotheses
- Evidence
- Observations

Readers should be able to trace every major conclusion.

---

# Relationship to AI

AI may assist with:

- Drafting summaries
- Organizing findings
- Identifying references

Researchers remain responsible for ensuring reports accurately represent the underlying research.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful report enables readers to:

- Understand the research.
- Trust the conclusions.
- Locate supporting evidence.
- Identify research limitations.
- Build upon previous work.
