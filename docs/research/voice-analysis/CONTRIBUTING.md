# Contributing

## Purpose

This document describes how researchers contribute to the Voice Analysis framework.

Contributions may improve the methodology, expand research, refine existing models, or strengthen the overall quality of the project.

Every contribution should support the framework's goals of reproducibility, traceability, and continuous improvement.

---

# Guiding Principles

Contributors should:

- Follow the established research workflow.
- Apply the methodology consistently.
- Document their reasoning.
- Preserve traceability.
- Respect existing research artifacts.
- Record lessons learned.

The objective is to improve the framework while maintaining the integrity of previous work.

---

# Types of Contributions

Examples include:

- New case studies
- Additional corpus sources
- Observations
- Evidence
- Hypotheses
- Validation
- Voice Models
- Research reports
- Experiments
- Methodology improvements
- Documentation improvements
- Framework enhancements

---

# Research Contributions

Research should:

- Begin with a defined objective.
- Identify the corpus being studied.
- Follow the documented workflow.
- Preserve supporting evidence.
- Document conclusions.
- Record limitations and open questions.

Research artifacts should remain traceable from source material through final conclusions.

---

# Methodology Contributions

Methodology improvements should be based on practical experience.

Contributors are encouraged to document:

- The problem encountered.
- The proposed improvement.
- The reasoning behind the change.
- The expected benefits.
- Any tradeoffs introduced.

Completed experiments provide the strongest foundation for improving the methodology.

---

# Documentation Contributions

Documentation should:

- Be clear and concise.
- Use consistent terminology.
- Follow established project conventions.
- Reference related research artifacts where appropriate.

Documentation should improve understanding without introducing unnecessary complexity.

---

# Reviewing Contributions

Before considering work complete, review the contribution for:

- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Traceability
- Consistency
- Clarity

Research should remain understandable to future contributors.

---

# Versioning

Significant contributions should be documented through the project's established versioning and decision-record process.

Major framework changes should include the reasoning behind the decision.

---

# Collaboration

Researchers are encouraged to:

- Share observations.
- Question assumptions.
- Discuss alternative interpretations.
- Improve existing work.
- Build upon previous research.

Constructive collaboration strengthens both the research and the framework.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful contribution:

- Advances the project's research goals.
- Preserves research quality.
- Improves the framework where appropriate.
- Remains understandable to future contributors.
- Leaves the project stronger than it was before the contribution.
