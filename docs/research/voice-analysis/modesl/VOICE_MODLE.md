# Voice Model

## Purpose

This document defines the structure of a completed Voice Model within the Voice Analysis methodology.

A Voice Model is the primary research artifact produced by the methodology.

It represents the current understanding of an author's writing voice based on validated hypotheses supported by documented evidence.

A Voice Model is not a prompt.

It is not a style guide.

It is not intended to imitate an author.

It is a research-backed representation of how an author consistently communicates.

---

# Relationship to the Research Process

The Voice Model is produced only after research has progressed through the complete methodology.

```text
Corpus
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Evidence
    ↓
Hypothesis
    ↓
Validation
    ↓
Voice Model
```

The Voice Model summarizes validated research.

It does not replace the supporting evidence.

---

# Characteristics

A Voice Model should be:

- Evidence-based
- Traceable
- Reproducible
- Explainable
- Versioned
- Continuously improvable

Every significant conclusion should be traceable back to documented hypotheses and supporting evidence.

---

# Recommended Structure

## Overview

A high-level summary describing the author's writing voice.

This section should provide a concise introduction to the author's communication style without attempting to capture every detail.

---

## Identity

Describe the author's overall communication identity.

Possible topics include:

- Intended audience
- Communication goals
- Typical perspective
- Level of formality
- Relationship with the reader

---

## Organization

Describe how ideas are typically organized.

Examples include:

- Opening patterns
- Narrative progression
- Explanation order
- Use of transitions
- Closing techniques

---

## Language

Describe recurring language characteristics.

Possible observations include:

- Vocabulary
- Sentence structure
- Paragraph length
- Repetition
- Contractions
- Technical terminology
- Idioms
- Conversational language

---

## Narrative Style

Describe storytelling characteristics.

Examples:

- Personal anecdotes
- Historical context
- Humor within stories
- Story placement
- Story length

---

## Teaching Style

Describe how the author explains ideas.

Possible characteristics include:

- Building from examples
- Incremental complexity
- Use of analogies
- Definitions
- Step-by-step explanations
- Practical demonstrations

---

## Humor

Describe recurring humor patterns.

Possible topics:

- Frequency
- Timing
- Delivery
- Self-deprecation
- Running jokes
- Sarcasm
- Wordplay

---

## Reader Interaction

Describe how the author engages readers.

Examples include:

- Questions
- Direct address
- Invitations
- Challenges
- Shared assumptions
- Encouragement

---

## Signature Characteristics

Identify writing behaviors that consistently distinguish the author.

These characteristics should represent highly validated observations rather than isolated examples.

---

## Evolution

Describe how the author's writing changes over time.

Possible topics include:

- Increasing technical depth
- Changes in tone
- Changes in humor
- Changes in structure
- Audience shifts
- Writing maturity

A Voice Model should acknowledge that writing is not static.

---

## Known Exceptions

Document situations where the Voice Model is less applicable.

Examples may include:

- Professional writing
- Academic writing
- Marketing material
- Short-form social media
- Highly emotional writing

Exceptions strengthen the model by defining its boundaries.

---

# Traceability

Every section of the Voice Model should reference supporting research.

Possible references include:

- Supporting hypotheses
- Confidence level
- Evidence identifiers
- Relevant observations

Researchers should be able to trace every significant conclusion back to its original source.

---

# Versioning

Voice Models should evolve over time.

Each published version should include:

- Version number
- Date
- Corpus scope
- Significant changes
- Outstanding research questions

Older versions should be preserved for historical comparison.

---

# Relationship to Derived Outputs

The Voice Model serves as the foundation for additional research products.

Potential derived outputs include:

- Style Guides
- AI Prompt Frameworks
- Writing Analysis Reports
- Comparative Author Studies
- Educational Materials
- Voice Evolution Reports

Derived outputs should reference the Voice Model rather than bypassing the research process.

---

# Relationship to AI

Artificial intelligence may assist in:

- Summarizing findings
- Organizing model sections
- Generating candidate descriptions
- Comparing model revisions

AI does not define the Voice Model.

The Voice Model is a human-reviewed research artifact built upon validated evidence.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Voice Model should enable another researcher to:

- Understand the author's writing characteristics.
- Explain why each conclusion exists.
- Trace conclusions back to supporting evidence.
- Identify the limits of the model.
- Continue improving the model as additional research becomes available.

The Voice Model should represent the current best understanding of an author's writing while remaining open to future refinement.
