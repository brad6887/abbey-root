# Corpus Model

## Purpose

This document defines how corpora are represented within the Voice Analysis methodology.

A corpus is the complete collection of source material available for research.

The quality, diversity, and documentation of the corpus directly influence the quality of every observation, hypothesis, and Voice Model derived from it.

The corpus is the foundation of all research.

---

# Definition

A corpus is a documented collection of writing produced by a single author or group of authors.

A corpus may include:

- Social media posts
- Emails
- Articles
- Books
- Documentation
- Blog posts
- Forum discussions
- Technical writing
- Personal journals
- Interview transcripts

The methodology is intentionally independent of any specific source.

---

# Corpus Principles

## Immutable Source Material

Original source material should remain unchanged.

Cleaning, formatting, and annotation should occur separately from the original data whenever practical.

---

## Traceability

Every document within a corpus should be traceable to its original source.

Researchers should be able to answer:

- Where did this come from?
- When was it written?
- Has it been modified?

---

## Metadata

Each corpus should document:

- Author
- Source
- Date range
- Approximate size
- Language
- Collection method
- Known limitations

---

## Completeness

Researchers should document whether the corpus is:

- Complete
- Representative
- Sampled
- Partial
- Unknown

Conclusions should always consider corpus quality.

---

## Diversity

Researchers should understand the variety contained within the corpus.

Examples include:

- Different years
- Different audiences
- Different writing purposes
- Different emotional contexts
- Different publication formats

A diverse corpus generally produces stronger research.

---

# Corpus Record

Each corpus should include:

- Corpus ID
- Name
- Description
- Author
- Date range
- Source(s)
- Size
- Metadata
- Known limitations

---

# Corpus Quality

Researchers should evaluate:

- Coverage
- Authenticity
- Consistency
- Diversity
- Potential bias
- Missing periods

Corpus quality should be discussed before significant research begins.

---

# Relationship to Experiments

Experiments operate on defined portions of a corpus.

Every experiment should identify exactly which corpus was used.

---

# Relationship to AI

AI may assist with:

- Corpus organization
- Metadata extraction
- Duplicate detection
- Preliminary classification

Researchers remain responsible for determining corpus suitability.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful corpus is:

- Well documented
- Traceable
- Representative of the research objective
- Easy for future researchers to understand
