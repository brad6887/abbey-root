# Glossary

## Purpose

This document defines the terminology used throughout the Voice Analysis framework.

Consistent terminology improves communication, documentation, and reproducibility.

---

# AI

An artificial intelligence system that assists researchers with analysis, organization, summarization, and pattern recognition while operating within the Voice Analysis methodology.

---

# Author

The individual whose writing is being studied.

---

# Case Study

A complete research project that applies the methodology to a specific author or body of work.

---

# Confidence

An assessment of how strongly the available evidence supports a validated conclusion.

Confidence reflects the quality and consistency of the supporting research.

---

# Corpus

The complete collection of source material selected for research.

The corpus forms the foundation of every research project.

---

# Derived Output

Any artifact created from the completed Voice Model.

Examples include style guides, AI prompts, summaries, comparative analyses, and reports.

---

# Evidence

A documented collection of related observations that supports a recurring characteristic within the author's writing.

Evidence provides the foundation for developing hypotheses.

---

# Experiment

A structured application of the methodology intended to answer a research question or evaluate an aspect of the framework.

---

# Framework

The complete collection of principles, methodology, workflows, models, and supporting documentation that define the Voice Analysis project.

---

# Hypothesis

A proposed explanation for a recurring pattern identified through evidence.

Hypotheses are evaluated through validation before becoming part of the Voice Model.

---

# Methodology

The standardized process used to conduct research within the framework.

---

# Model

A structured representation of research findings or framework concepts.

Examples include the Corpus Model, Evidence Model, Validation Model, and Voice Model.

---

# Observation

An objective description of something identified within the corpus.

Observations record what is present without assigning explanation or meaning.

---

# Principle

A foundational belief that guides research decisions throughout the framework.

---

# Report

A structured summary of completed research intended to communicate findings and conclusions.

---

# Research Artifact

Any documented output created during the research process.

Examples include observations, evidence, hypotheses, validation records, reports, and Voice Models.

---

# Research Workflow

The operational process followed to conduct research using the methodology.

---

# Source Material

Original content created by the author that forms part of the corpus.

---

# Style Guide

A practical reference derived from the Voice Model that assists writers or AI systems in reproducing aspects of an author's writing style.

---

# Traceability

The ability to connect any research conclusion back to its supporting evidence and source material.

---

# Validation

The structured process of evaluating hypotheses against available evidence and contradictory examples.

---

# Voice Model

The primary research output that describes an author's communication style based on validated research findings.
