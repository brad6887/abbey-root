# Voice Analysis Ideas

This document captures ideas, experiments, and future directions that arise during the project.

Items in this document are intentionally **not commitments**. They exist to preserve ideas without interrupting the current research.

The methodology and current case study take precedence over this list.

---

# Future Case Studies

- Analyze additional authors to validate the methodology.
- Compare authors with distinctly different writing styles.
- Analyze collaborative writing.
- Compare professional writing with personal writing.
- Compare public writing with private journals (where available).

---

Explore objective measurements such as:

- Sentence length
- Paragraph length
- Vocabulary diversity
- Word frequency
- Reading level
- Punctuation usage
- Emoji usage
- Dialogue frequency
- Question frequency
- Passive vs active voice
- Pronoun usage
- Contraction frequency

# Research Questions

Questions worth exploring:

- What distinguishes voice from style?
- Can voice be measured objectively?
- Which characteristics are stable across decades?
- How much writing is required to model a voice?
- Can multiple researchers independently reach similar conclusions?
- Which observations are measurable?
- Which observations remain inherently subjective?

---

# Long-Term Vision

Develop a general methodology for modeling an author's writing voice that is:

- Evidence-based
- Explainable
- Reproducible
- Transparent
- Independent of any particular AI model

The long-term objective is to produce a research framework that can be applied to any sufficiently large corpus of writing.
