# Hypothesis

## Description

Within authored Facebook posts containing quotation marks, the author
recurrently uses the marked wording as a stance marker rather than only as
neutral punctuation.

The core stance-marker function signals that a term is disputed, nonliteral,
inadequate, provisional, or being replaced with a deliberately comic name.

This function should remain observable after ordinary titles, attributed
quotations, reported speech, copied material, and the canonical EVID-004
examples are separated from the validation population.

## Status

Proposed

## Supporting Evidence

- OBS-004, Version 2
- EVID-004
- REVIEW-004

The complete candidate review found:

- 44 retained scare-or-distancing cases.
- 32 retained invented-label-or-renaming cases.
- 20 provisional direct-quotation or reported-speech cases.
- 65 retained comparison cases.
- 4 rejected or unsuitable matches.

EVID-004 selected a conservative cross-period canonical set from the two core
supporting categories.

## Comparison and Contradictory Evidence

The corpus contains many quote-bearing posts whose quotation marks perform an
ordinary function:

- song, film, product, and work titles,
- attributed external quotations,
- reported or overheard speech,
- copied prompts,
- and malformed punctuation.

These cases contradict any broader claim that quotation marks generally
signal comic stance.

They do not contradict the narrower hypothesis that stance marking is one
recurring and separable function within quote-bearing authored posts.

## Operational Definitions

### Core Supporting Case

A post is a core supporting case when quotation marks materially:

- distance the writer from a term or label,
- signal that wording should not be accepted literally,
- mark a conventional description as inadequate,
- introduce a deliberately altered or invented name,
- or frame a phrase for literal reinterpretation or reversal.

REVIEW-004 codes:

- `SD-S`
- `IR-S`

### Comparison Case

A post is a comparison when quotation marks primarily mark:

- an ordinary title or proper name,
- a neutral direct quotation,
- neutral reported speech,
- or another function that does not materially create the stance described
  above.

### Excluded Case

Copied, platform-derived, malformed, or otherwise unsuitable text is excluded
from the validation denominator.

## Independent Validation Population

VAL-004 should use the deterministic 165-post quote-bearing candidate set but
must remove:

- the 15 canonical supporting examples cited in EVID-004,
- the 7 canonical comparison examples cited in EVID-004,
- and the 4 rejected matches recorded in REVIEW-004.

The resulting holdout population must be derived by source identifier before
calculating results.

The expected holdout size is 139 sources:

```text
165 candidates - 15 canonical support - 7 canonical comparisons
- 4 rejected matches = 139
```

VAL-004 must stop if the derived cardinality differs.

Provisional direct-quotation and reported-speech support must not count toward
the core rate unless independently re-reviewed into one of the two core
categories.

## Testable Predictions

### Core Rate

At least 30% of the eligible holdout population will be classified as
`SD-S` or `IR-S`.

### Subtype Replication

The holdout will contain at least:

- 10 scare-or-distancing cases,
- and 10 invented-label-or-renaming cases.

This prevents the result from depending on only one rhetorical subtype.

### Cross-Period Recurrence

Core supporting cases will appear in at least three of these four
chronological bands:

1. 2009 through 2011
2. 2012 through 2014
3. 2015 through 2017
4. 2018 through 2021

Posts after 2021 may be reported but are not required because quote-bearing
eligible material is sparse in the later corpus.

### Boundary Preservation

The holdout must retain at least 20 comparison cases. This verifies that the
method distinguishes the proposed function from ordinary quotation rather
than treating quotation marks themselves as sufficient evidence.

## Falsification Criteria

HYP-004 will be rejected or revised if any of these occur:

- The core rate is below 30%.
- Either core subtype has fewer than 10 holdout cases.
- Core cases appear in fewer than three chronological bands.
- Fewer than 20 comparison cases remain, indicating that the candidate
  construction or classification boundary is not meaningful.
- Exact-citation or source-set validation fails.

If the aggregate threshold passes but one subtype fails, the hypothesis should
be narrowed to the surviving subtype rather than treated as fully supported.

## Confidence

Medium

Confidence is medium for the existence of the stance-marker function within
Facebook writing because the evidence review covers every deterministic
quote-bearing candidate and preserves ordinary comparisons.

Confidence remains low for prevalence among all posts, comparative
distinctiveness, intent, reader response, and transfer beyond Facebook.

## Open Questions

- Does distancing occur more consistently than comic renaming?
- Do single and double quotation marks serve different functions?
- Does the same stance marking occur through capitalization, italics, or
  wording without quotation marks?
- Does the pattern occur in technical or long-form writing?
- Is the pattern distinctive compared with other Facebook writers?
