# Observation

## Description

Within Facebook posts that contain a discernible absurd, impossible, fictional, or strongly exaggerated humorous premise, the author frequently uses ordinary declarative language rather than explicitly emphasizing that the situation is unusual.

The unusual element is often treated as a normal part of the conversation.

## Supporting Characteristics

Observed characteristics:

- Absurd or impossible elements are introduced without explanation.
- The writing remains conversational even when the subject matter is unusual.
- The contrast between the situation and the presentation style is a recurring characteristic.

## Limitations

This observation describes a recurring pattern found in the initial Facebook Corpus experiment.

Prevalence measurement found:

- 11 deadpan posts in a deterministic 140-post sample.
- 16 sampled posts with a qualifying absurd-humorous premise.
- A 68.75% conditional deadpan rate.
- A 95% Wilson interval of 44.40% to 85.84%.

It does not yet determine:

- Frequency outside the sampled Facebook corpus.
- Intent behind the writing choice.
- Whether the characteristic is unique to the author.
- Whether the pattern appears consistently across different writing formats.

## Questions Raised

- Is deadpan delivery present throughout the corpus or primarily in early Facebook posts?
- Does deadpan delivery occur more frequently with recurring fictional elements?
- Is deadpan delivery used equally in short and long-form writing?
