# Observation

## Description

The author frequently communicates complete ideas using highly compressed language.

Writing often consists of short sentences, sentence fragments, or single words that rely on shared context and reader inference to convey meaning.

The writing achieves completeness without requiring extensive explanation.

## Supporting Characteristics

Observed characteristics:

- Complete thoughts are frequently expressed using very few words.
- Single-word responses may communicate context, reaction, or opinion.
- The writing often depends on the reader understanding implied context.
- Longer posts tend to use compressed phrases rather than extended explanation.
- Additional detail is added when required by the idea rather than as a default writing style.

## Limitations

This observation describes a recurring pattern found in the initial Facebook Corpus experiment.

It does not yet determine:

- Whether concise expression remains consistent across the complete corpus.
- Whether the pattern appears in other writing formats.
- Whether brevity is a deliberate stylistic choice or a natural communication pattern.
- Whether the characteristic varies based on topic, audience, or emotional context.

## Questions Raised

- Does concise expression remain consistent across different time periods?
- Does the same pattern appear in technical writing and documentation?
- Are longer posts exceptions driven by subject complexity?
- Does the author rely more heavily on reader inference than explicit explanation?
- Are there contexts where the author intentionally uses extended narrative?
