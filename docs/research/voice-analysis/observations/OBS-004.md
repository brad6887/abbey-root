# Observation

## Description

The author sometimes uses quotation marks to create distance from the literal
meaning of a term or label.

The quoted language may be treated as doubtful, provisional, renamed, or
reinterpreted. In humorous examples, the marked phrase often establishes the
target of a reversal or supplies a deliberately comic alternative name.

The characteristic is not the use of quotation marks by itself. Ordinary
titles, attributed quotations, and reported speech are comparison cases unless
the surrounding writing changes their function.

## Supporting Characteristics

Observed forms include:

- Marking a conventional label as questionable or inadequate.
- Giving an ordinary subject a deliberately altered or invented name.
- Reinterpreting a quoted phrase literally.
- Quoting a claim before supplying a reversal or consequence.
- Using marked language as a compact setup whose stance becomes clear in the
  surrounding sentence.

## Limitations

The deterministic search finds quote-bearing candidates, not the rhetorical
function of each quotation.

The complete candidate review classified all 165 quote-bearing candidates:

- 76 retained supporting cases,
- 20 provisional supporting cases,
- 65 retained comparisons,
- and 4 rejected matches.

The review does not yet determine:

- Whether distancing, renaming, and reversal should remain one observation or
  be split into subtypes.
- How frequently the pattern occurs relative to ordinary quotation.
- Whether the pattern is distinctive compared with other writers.
- Whether it appears in technical or long-form writing.

The local AI worker did not complete the original 42-source classification
batches. A later seventeen-batch run, limited to ten sources per batch, passed
exact completeness validation and is recorded in REVIEW-004.

## Questions Raised

- What proportion of quote-bearing posts use comic framing?
- Do distancing, renaming, and reversal show the same evidence distribution?
- Does the pattern persist across all chronological periods?
- Are single quotation marks used differently from double quotation marks?
- Does the same framing technique appear without visible quotation marks?
- Does the pattern transfer beyond Facebook writing?
