# Principles

## Purpose

This document defines the core principles that guide every aspect of the Voice Analysis framework.

These principles provide a foundation for making research decisions, evolving the methodology, and evaluating future improvements.

When the methodology does not prescribe a specific action, these principles provide guidance.

---

# 1. Evidence First

Research begins with evidence.

Observations, hypotheses, and models are built from documented source material rather than assumptions or intuition.

Every meaningful conclusion should be supported by traceable evidence.

---

# 2. Objectivity

Research should describe the author's writing as it exists.

Researchers should distinguish between observation, interpretation, and personal opinion throughout the research process.

---

# 3. Traceability

Every research artifact should be traceable to its supporting sources.

Future researchers should be able to understand how conclusions were reached and reproduce the research process.

Traceability strengthens confidence in the resulting models.

---

# 4. Reproducibility

The methodology should produce consistent results when applied by different researchers to the same corpus.

Standardized workflows, documentation, and research artifacts improve consistency across experiments.

---

# 5. Continuous Learning

Every experiment contributes to both research findings and the improvement of the methodology.

The framework grows through practical experience and documented lessons learned.

---

# 6. Simplicity

Simple processes are easier to understand, reproduce, and improve.

The framework should favor straightforward solutions that accomplish the research objective while remaining flexible enough to accommodate future growth.

---

# 7. Transparency

Research decisions should be visible and well documented.

Assumptions, uncertainties, revisions, and limitations should be recorded throughout the research process.

Transparency allows others to understand both the strengths and the boundaries of the research.

---

# 8. Curiosity

Research should encourage exploration.

Unexpected observations, contradictory evidence, and new questions are valuable outcomes that often lead to deeper understanding.

The framework should support discovery rather than restrict it.

---

# 9. Continuous Improvement

The methodology is expected to evolve as experience grows.

Framework improvements should be informed by completed experiments, researcher feedback, and demonstrated opportunities for improvement.

Deliberate evolution strengthens the framework over time.

---

# 10. Stewardship

Research should be organized, preserved, and maintained as a long-term knowledge asset.

Documentation, evidence, and conclusions should remain understandable and useful to future researchers.

The framework is designed to accumulate knowledge rather than simply complete individual studies.

---

# Applying the Principles

These principles should guide decisions involving:

- Research planning
- Corpus selection
- Observation techniques
- Evidence evaluation
- Hypothesis development
- Validation
- Framework evolution
- AI-assisted research

When multiple approaches appear valid, researchers should select the approach that best reflects these principles.

---

# Success Criteria

The Principles are successful when they:

- Guide consistent research decisions.
- Support objective and evidence-based analysis.
- Encourage reproducible research.
- Promote thoughtful evolution of the framework.
- Preserve the long-term integrity of the Voice Analysis project.
