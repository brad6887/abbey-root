# Validation

## Hypothesis

HYP-004 proposes that, within authored Facebook posts containing quotation
marks, the author recurrently uses marked wording as a stance marker.

The core function signals that wording is disputed, nonliteral, inadequate,
provisional, or being replaced with a deliberately comic name.

## Validation Objective

Determine whether the two core stance-marker subtypes remain recurrent after
removing:

- all canonical EVID-004 supporting examples,
- all canonical EVID-004 comparisons,
- and all rejected candidate matches.

The validation must preserve ordinary quotation comparisons and pass exact
source-integrity checks.

## Current Assessment

HYP-004 is supported within the tested Facebook scope.

Independent holdout evidence shows that quotation marks recurrently function
as stance markers for disputed, nonliteral, inadequate, or deliberately
renamed wording. Both distancing and renaming replicate after removing the
canonical evidence set.

The large comparison population confirms that the pattern is selective rather
than a property of all quotation-mark use.

The result does not establish:

- prevalence among all Facebook posts,
- recurrence after 2021,
- comparative distinctiveness,
- authorial intent,
- reader response,
- or transfer to technical and long-form writing.

## Outcome

Provisionally Supported

## Confidence

Medium

Confidence is medium for the existence, subtype replication, and cross-period
recurrence of the stance-marker function within quote-bearing Facebook posts
from 2009 through 2021.

Confidence remains low outside that population and for later periods.

## Open Questions

- Why does the holdout frequency decline in the 2018–2021 band?
- Does the pattern continue after 2021 in writing without quotation marks?
- Are distancing and comic renaming better modeled as separate voice
  characteristics?
- Does the technique occur in technical or long-form writing?
- Is the rate distinctive relative to another writer's Facebook corpus?
