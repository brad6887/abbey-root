## Recommended Next Observations

### Quoted language as a comic framing device

**Priority:** High

**Candidates:** B001-C02, B004-C01

Quotation marks appear in two distinct roles: casting doubt on an ordinary
label and importing a recognizable outside phrase. The common possibility is
that quoted language creates distance between the literal wording and the
post's stance.

**Next test:** Review quoted phrases across the full eligible corpus and
separate skeptical emphasis, direct quotation, titles, and ordinary
punctuation.

### Hashtags as punchlines or closing commentary

**Priority:** High

**Candidates:** B003-C03, B011-C01

Two widely separated batches independently identify hashtags added within or
after otherwise complete statements. The late placement may function as a
secondary comment rather than simple categorization.

**Next test:** Enumerate hashtag-bearing posts, code placement and function,
and compare descriptive tags with comic or evaluative closers.

### Reusable first-person opening templates

**Priority:** Medium

**Candidates:** B002-C03, B003-C02, B007-C02

The worker found recurring `I want`, `I wish`, and `I just` openings in three
periods. This may reflect a broader preference for compact first-person setup
templates, but the constructions may also be ordinary status conventions.

**Next test:** Compare these openings with other first-person posts and test
whether the remainder of the sentence carries distinctive compression,
reversal, or humor.
