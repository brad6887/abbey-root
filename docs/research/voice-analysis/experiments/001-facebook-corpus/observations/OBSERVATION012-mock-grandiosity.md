# Observation 012 – Mock Grandiosity

## Question

Does the author frequently describe ordinary events using language typically reserved for significant or historic occasions?

---

## Corpus

**Experiment:** 001 – Facebook Corpus

**Sample:**

Posts 101–200 from the normalized corpus.

---

## Method

Posts were reviewed for examples in which commonplace events were intentionally described using exaggerated, formal, or dramatic language.

The analysis focused on narrative framing rather than the underlying event itself.

---

## Findings

Multiple posts elevate routine experiences through intentionally disproportionate language.

Examples include:

- Referring to a confrontation with an opossum as a battle with no winners.
- Declaring that a skunk "paid the ultimate price."
- Announcing imaginary enemies should surrender before a holiday trip to Texas.
- Suggesting a People Magazine award should arrive in the mail.
- Treating removing mattress tags as an act of living dangerously.

In each case, the underlying event remains ordinary while the narration adopts the tone of an important historical or dramatic event.

---

## Interpretation

The author frequently creates humor by increasing the narrative importance of otherwise unremarkable situations.

Rather than exaggerating the event itself, the writing exaggerates the significance assigned to it.

This technique allows everyday experiences to become humorous through contrast between the event and its presentation.

---

## Questions Raised

- Does mock grandiosity remain common throughout the corpus?
- Is this technique more common in autobiographical writing than fictional writing?
- How often does mock grandiosity overlap with deadpan delivery?
- Does this technique become more or less frequent over time?
