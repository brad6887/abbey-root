# Observation 001 – Facebook Status Prompt Artifact

## Question

Does the Facebook interface introduce artifacts that distort analysis of natural opening sentence structure?

---

## Corpus

**Experiment:** 001 – Facebook Corpus

**Sample:**

Early Facebook status updates from the normalized corpus.

---

## Method

Early posts were reviewed to determine whether opening phrases originated from the author's writing or from Facebook's built-in "Brad Cooke is..." status prompt.

Posts whose opening text completed the interface-generated prompt were identified as corpus artifacts rather than independent stylistic choices.

---

## Findings

Many early Facebook status updates begin with phrases such as:

- is rebooting
- is confused
- is drinking coffee
- is sneezing

These openings complete Facebook's built-in status prompt rather than representing independently authored opening sentences.

As a result, the opening words of many early posts reflect the design of the Facebook interface rather than the author's natural writing style.

---

## Interpretation

The Facebook status prompt introduces a systematic artifact into the corpus that can bias analyses of sentence openings and opening-word frequency.

This characteristic is a property of the source material rather than the author's writing style and should be considered during corpus construction and stylistic analysis.

---

## Questions Raised

- Should these interface-generated openings be normalized during corpus construction?
- Should opening-sentence analyses exclude early status-style posts?
- Do other Facebook interface conventions introduce similar artifacts elsewhere in the corpus?
