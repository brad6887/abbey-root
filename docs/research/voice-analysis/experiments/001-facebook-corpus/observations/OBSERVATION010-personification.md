# Observation 010 – Personification of Objects and Systems

## Question

Does the author frequently attribute human characteristics or agency to objects, systems, or abstract concepts?

---

## Corpus

**Experiment:** 001 – Facebook Corpus

**Sample:**

The first 100 chronological posts from the normalized corpus.

---

## Method

Posts were reviewed for examples in which non-human entities were described as thinking, acting, communicating, or otherwise behaving like people.

The observation focuses on writing technique rather than humorous intent.

---

## Findings

Multiple posts describe objects, technology, or abstract concepts as though they possess human qualities or intentions.

Rather than treating these entities as passive objects, the writing often presents them as active participants in the situation.

This technique contributes to the conversational and imaginative tone of the writing while requiring minimal explanation.

---

## Interpretation

The author appears comfortable blurring the distinction between people and non-human entities when constructing narratives.

Personification provides an efficient way to establish fictional situations without lengthy exposition.

---

## Questions Raised

- Does personification appear consistently throughout the corpus?
- Is it primarily associated with humorous writing?
- Which categories of objects or systems are personified most frequently?
