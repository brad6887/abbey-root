# Observation 008 – Single-Premise Construction

## Question

Does the author typically build each humorous post around a single central premise rather than combining multiple unrelated ideas?

---

## Corpus

**Experiment:** 001 – Facebook Corpus

**Sample:**

The first 100 chronological posts from the normalized corpus.

---

## Method

Humorous posts within the sample were reviewed to determine whether they relied on a single primary concept or multiple independent concepts.

The structure of each post was evaluated rather than the quality or effectiveness of the humor.

---

## Findings

Most humorous posts introduce one central premise and remain focused on developing that single idea.

Examples include fictional situations, impossible events, or cultural references that serve as the sole foundation of the post.

Additional jokes or unrelated concepts are rarely introduced before the post concludes.

This results in concise writing with a clear narrative focus.

---

## Interpretation

The author appears to favor developing one concept completely rather than layering multiple independent jokes into the same piece of writing.

This approach reduces cognitive load for the reader while allowing the primary premise to remain the focal point.

---

## Questions Raised

- Does this structure persist in longer writing?
- Is the single-premise approach used equally in serious writing?
- Are exceptions associated with storytelling rather than short-form status updates?
