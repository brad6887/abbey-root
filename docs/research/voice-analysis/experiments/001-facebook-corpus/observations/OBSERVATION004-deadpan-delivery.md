# Observation 004 – Deadpan Delivery

## Question

Does the author consistently present absurd or impossible situations using a serious, matter-of-fact tone?

---

## Corpus

**Experiment:** 001 – Facebook Corpus

**Sample:**

The first 100 chronological posts from the normalized corpus.

---

## Method

Posts containing absurd, impossible, or highly improbable statements were reviewed.

The tone of each post was evaluated to determine whether the absurdity was emphasized or simply presented as an ordinary statement.

---

## Findings

Many humorous posts present impossible situations without announcing that they are jokes.

Examples include:

- Being lost in space.
- Rebooting.
- Installing a flux capacitor.
- Successfully traveling two hours into the future.
- Putting out fires with gasoline.

The writing generally avoids emotional language, exaggerated punctuation, or explicit signals that the statement is intended humorously.

Instead, impossible events are described using the same tone that would normally be used to describe everyday activities.

---

## Interpretation

The humor often depends on the contrast between an impossible premise and an entirely ordinary narrative voice.

Rather than telling the reader something is ridiculous, the writing allows the absurdity of the situation to create the humor.

---

## Questions Raised

- Is deadpan delivery present throughout the corpus or primarily in early Facebook posts?
- Does deadpan delivery occur more frequently with recurring fictional elements?
- Is deadpan delivery used equally in short and long-form writing?
