# Observation 014 – Authorial Restraint

## Question

Does the author consistently avoid explaining, emphasizing, or evaluating the intended effect of a post?

---

## Corpus

**Experiment:** 001 – Facebook Corpus

**Sample:**

Posts 101–200 from the normalized corpus.

---

## Method

Posts were reviewed to determine whether the author explicitly explained the intended humor, emotional response, or significance of the writing.

The analysis focused on the amount of interpretation provided by the author versus the amount left to the reader.

---

## Findings

Most posts present an idea or situation without explaining why it is humorous, unusual, or important.

The writing rarely includes statements that tell the reader how to react or what conclusion to draw.

Instead, the post typically ends once the central idea has been presented.

Readers are expected to determine the significance or humor independently.

---

## Interpretation

The author appears to prefer presenting ideas without editorial commentary.

Rather than directing the audience's interpretation, the writing leaves space for readers to arrive at their own conclusions.

This restraint complements concise writing, deadpan delivery, and implicit reader participation.

---

## Questions Raised

- Does authorial restraint remain consistent in longer-form writing?
- Is explanatory language more common in serious or instructional writing?
- How closely does authorial restraint correlate with deadpan delivery?
